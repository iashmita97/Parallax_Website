Career's Journey

Born on June 24, 1987, in Rosario, Argentina.Diagnosed with a growth hormone deficiency at age 11. Joined Newell's Old Boys youth team at age 7. Moved to Barcelona at 13 after the club offered to pay for his treatment. Joined La Masia, Barcelona’s famous youth academy. Made his senior debut for Barcelona in 2004 at age 17. Scored his first senior goal in 2005 vs Albacete. Became a Barcelona regular by 2006–2007 season. Wore the iconic number 10 jersey after Ronaldinho's departure. Won his first Ballon d'Or in 2009. Helped Barcelona win the sextuple in 2009 (6 trophies in one year). Became Barcelona's all-time top scorer in 2012. Won 4 Champions League titles with Barcelona. Scored 91 goals in 2012, a world record for a calendar year. Reached 3 Copa America finals and 1 World Cup final before winning. Won Copa America 2021, his first major international title with Argentina. Won the FIFA World Cup 2022 in Qatar, fulfilling his lifelong dream. Left Barcelona in 2021 due to financial issues; joined Paris Saint-Germain (PSG). Won Ligue 1 titles with PSG before leaving in 2023. Joined Inter Miami in Major League Soccer (MLS) and continues his career there.

EXPLORE ABOUT HIS FAMILY!

Family

Lionel Messi was born into a close-knit and humble family in Rosario, Argentina. His father, Jorge Messi, worked as a steel factory manager, while his mother, Celia Cuccittini, was employed as a part-time cleaner. Messi is the third of four children, with two older brothers, Rodrigo and Matías, and a younger sister, María Sol. From a young age, his family recognized his exceptional talent and supported his football dreams wholeheartedly. His grandmother, Celia, played a particularly important role in his early life and used to accompany him to his matches. Messi often dedicates his goals to her, pointing to the sky in her memory. The Messi family made a life-changing decision when they moved to Barcelona to support Lionel’s treatment and career. His father stayed by his side during the difficult transition, acting as his first agent and main supporter. Throughout his rise to fame, Messi has maintained strong ties with his family, often spending holidays and special occasions with them. His siblings have also been involved in various aspects of his business and career management. Messi married his childhood sweetheart, Antonela Roccuzzo, in 2017 in Rosario. The couple has known each other since they were children, as Antonela is the cousin of Messi's close friend. They have three sons together: Thiago, Mateo, and Ciro, and Messi often speaks about how fatherhood has changed his perspective on life. Antonela plays a supportive and grounding role in his life, often seen cheering him on at matches. The Messi family is known for keeping a low profile despite Lionel’s fame, preferring privacy and simplicity. They frequently share moments of joy and travel together, showing the importance of togetherness. Messi’s family remains one of his greatest sources of strength and inspiration, shaping the humble personality that endears him to fans around the world.

LOOK, DEEP INTO HIS ACHIEVEMENTS

Achievements

Lionel Messi's football career is decorated with historic achievements that place him among the greatest players of all time. He has won the **Ballon d’Or** a record **eight times**, more than any other player in history. With **Barcelona**, he won **10 La Liga titles**, **7 Copa del Rey titles**, and **4 UEFA Champions League trophies**, making him the club’s most successful player. Messi is **Barcelona’s all-time leading scorer** with 672 goals in official matches. In 2012, he set a world record by scoring **91 goals in a single calendar year**, surpassing Gerd Müller’s long-standing record. He has also won the **Golden Shoe** (Europe’s top scorer) **six times**. Internationally, Messi won **Olympic gold** with Argentina in **2008** and the **FIFA U-20 World Cup** in **2005**. After years of heartbreak, he led Argentina to victory in the **Copa America 2021**, his first senior international title. His greatest achievement came in **2022**, when he captained Argentina to win the **FIFA World Cup** in Qatar, also winning the **Golden Ball** for best player of the tournament. He became the **first player to score in every knockout round** of a World Cup. He is also **Argentina’s all-time top scorer**, surpassing Gabriel Batistuta. With **Paris Saint-Germain**, Messi won **two Ligue 1 titles** and continued breaking assist records. He became the **only player to register 20+ goals and assists** in a single European league season multiple times. In 2023, Messi joined **Inter Miami** and led them to their **first-ever trophy**, the **Leagues Cup**. He is the **first player in history** to win the Ballon d'Or while playing outside Europe. Messi also holds the record for the **most goals in a single European club (Barcelona)**. His influence extends beyond numbers; he’s admired for his humility, vision, and unmatched dribbling skills. Lionel Messi’s legacy is one of consistency, brilliance, and global inspiration.
